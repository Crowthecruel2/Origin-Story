Faction

Children of Change

A cult that preaches mutation as sacrament. They run hidden labs and slumside "clinics," offering rebirth through painful transformation. The flesh is a chrysalis, and they will break it open.

Rumors & Myths

  • Each sanctuary has bone-carved words: "The flesh is a chrysalis. Break it."
  • Failed transformations are "blessed but unready" and recycled into biotech.
  • Some initiates pay to be reborn; others vanish into back-alley clinics.
  • Whispers say their leader no longer has a stable shape.

Lore (Public knowledge)

The Children worship Change as a cosmic force. Mutation is destiny, pain is transformation, and the human form is a cage. They promise transcendence to the desperate, the devout, and the obsessed.

How they operate

  • Run secret labs and sanctuaries across the undercity.
  • Offer mutagenic rites in exchange for loyalty.
  • Send scouts and zealots to recruit the desperate.

Why they transform

  • Humanity is incomplete; evolution must be forced.
  • Suffering is sacred because it births new forms.
  • They seek the next species and call it salvation.

GM Section: Doctrine & Roles

Structure

  • The Gene-Lit: leaders so mutated they are barely human.
  • Fleshcallers: bioengineers and prophets running the rites.
  • Metamorphs: ascended mutants with varied forms.
  • Supplicants: initiates awaiting change.
  • Swarmkin: failed metamorphs used as shock troops.

Rites

  • Rite of Unmaking: strip stagnant traits.
  • Rite of Becoming: apply mutagenic catalysts.
  • Rite of Emergence: survivors awaken changed.
  • Dead are "blessed but unready" and recycled into cult biotech.

GM Section: Hooks & Horror

Player interactions

  • Rescue recruits before rites begin.
  • Infiltrate a sanctuary mid-transformation cycle.
  • Negotiate with Fleshcallers for an antidote or mutation.

Adventure hooks

  • Rite Gone Wrong: a Becoming Storm spills into the streets.
  • Missing Scientist: a bioengineer joins the cult for "freedom."
  • Mutagen Trade: Undertow brokers a deal for cult catalysts.
