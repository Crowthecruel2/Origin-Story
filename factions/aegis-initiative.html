The Aegis Initiative

A corporate superhero program that sells justice as a luxury brand. Every hero is a product, every rescue is content, and the cameras roll even when the stakes are life or death.

Rumors & PR Whispers

  • Hero contracts include social quotas for posts, smiles, and scripted lines.
  • Body-cams run beautification filters in real time.
  • Containment teams scrub collateral damage before news crews arrive.
  • Executives with zero combat experience approve every mission.

Lore (Public knowledge)

Aegis markets "Justice. Refined." Heroism must be uplifting, apolitical, and perfectly on-brand. They fight the perception of crime as much as the crime itself, preserving public trust through consistency more than honesty.

How they operate

  • PR teams script rescues and pre-stage media angles.
  • Heroes are licensed assets with merch lines.
  • Risky missions get sanitized or outsourced.

Why they respond

  • Brand lift and trending moments.
  • High-value contracts and city sponsorships.
  • Control the narrative of heroism itself.

GM Section: Structure & Brand Machine

Divisions

  • Executive Board: profit-first strategists.
  • Heroic Division: Paragons, Spotlights, Brand Assets.
  • PR & Media: social feeds, merch, legal, image control.
  • Compliance & Risk: keeps heroes brand-safe.
  • Containment & Response: cleans up off-camera messes.

Tech & image

  • Lightweight tactical suits with glam filters.
  • Drone swarms filming every angle.
  • Hero-branded merch and sponsorships.
  • Curated feeds per operative to steer public mood.
